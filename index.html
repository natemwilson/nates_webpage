research experience

I am the lab manager for the Turk-Browne Lab at the Princeton Neuroscience Institute. We take a computational approach to cognitive psychology, using fMRI and behavioural methods to investigate the nature of learning, memory, and attention.

In 2013, I interned at the Cajal Institute with Dr. Maria L. de Ceballos. We studied the role of Arg-1 in Alzheimer's Disease using rodent models and immunohistochemistry.

During summer of 2012, I assisted a graduate project under the guidance Zemer Gitai at the Princeton Dept. of Molecular Biology. We looked at the role of enzyme co-localization in metabolism using molecular cloning techniques.

teaching experience

Over the 2013-2014 school year, I taught English class in a Spanish high school through the N.A.L.C.A. program.

I volunteered four hours per week over a six month period to teach English to native Spanish speaking adults with Catholic Charities Central Colorado.

For two academic years while in college, I tutored organic chemistry, biology, and calculus at the Colorado College Quantitative Reasoning Center.
